The Mechanics of Diffie-Hellman Encryption

Number of ways a message can be enciphered: Theoretically infinite, around 10^77 on most modern systems

Ease of encryption: High

Security: High*

Diffie-Hellman can be considered one of the first sophisticated "mathematical encryption" algorithms. It is based not just upon scrambling of letters and redirection of output, but upon one-way functions such as the natural logarithm. A function is generally said to be one-way if, given an output of the function, it is either infeasible or impossible to guess the input- these for functions are ideal for cryptography as, even if an encrypted message is intercepted, decoding it is extremely difficult.

The crucial thing to notice of this algorithm is that Alice and Bob never share their secret colors with each other. At no point is that information ever broadcast. Thus, even if an eavesdropper intercepted the mixture during the public transfer, they would have to brute-force their way through an almost innumerably vast number of possible keys to determine the secret colors Alice and Bob used.
